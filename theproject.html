The Project

How We Made Things a Bit Better

Configuring a Social Media Integration for Algonquin Trail Maps


This project was originally conceived because Provincial Parks in Ontario & a love for the outdoors was something that every Project Team member shares. So when the opportunity to develop a solution for Algonquin Park became a possibility, we decided to make it a reality. The current map for Algonquin Park and it's many services are showcased through the Ontario Provincial Park and we thought to encourage more user engagement we could configure a solution to integrate this service to their map.

Our Problem Statement:
‍
Identify your ideal state or outcome from completing the work.
To improve the engagement of trail users of Algonquin Provincial Park in Ontario.

Document the reality of how things are done/managed now.
The current interactive map inspiration: https://www.ontarioparks.com/park/
algonquin/maps. The current solution offers limited interactivity.

What are the consequences if your solution is not made/nothing is changed?
Status-quo results in the park losing opportunities to improve camper engagement with the trails in the park.

Clearly state the proposal solutions in the context of the end user.
Integrating social media to the existing interactive map and web page will allow users to view or share their experiences with other map viewers.

Our idea on how to create a solution for this problem was originally: to create an phone app for Android or Iphone. We looked into many different options to accomplish this but concluded that with a Mobile Configured website the task would be more achievable for this time frame. This site would include searchable individual trail information, as well as self-updating Social Media widgets (configured for the hashtags of each trail and the Park as a whole).  We are not using the original Ontario Parks web map as we do not have permissions or licensing to do so.

While some components could not be fully integrated to our solution and our original idea for a phone app changed, we still explored the options for layout, design and components for all items to solve the problem of lacking user engagement.

In this Slider are the concept sketches for what a future web application hosting our socially integrated map solution could look like. The app would be an all in one tool for visitors of Algonquin park; campsite bookings and faster check-in at the gate, Trail and exploration planning, canoe routes to download and use offline, and Interior expedition permits.
