The Plan Vs. The Reality

The Plan

Our Approach

For our group it was important to us to tackle the aspects of this project as a team while also playing to the strengths of each individual. To accomplish this we started off our project with a team meeting where we made a list of the different components that would need to be created or configured to create a usable solution that addressed our problem. From there we gave each component a category that the members could have input on the things they would like to be a part of and what unique skills or experience they could contribute to that component.

With the use of Microsoft Planner we divided these main components into blocks; then assigning the individual tasks within each to the group members who best fit with these blocks. It was also important for us to keep this organization flexible to allow for change if need be, and to encourage open dialogue.

These were the main tasks that needed to be accomplished to provide our finished solution:

  • Make A Plan - Using Microsoft Planner to keep all members on track with due dates and checklist items
  • Divide tasks and assign soft and hard deadlines to ensure tracked progress on the deliverables
  • Create layout and outline in Word to ensure all rubric items have been met in final deliverables
  • Map structure and layout - incorporating data and layers from constructed features in ArcMap
  • Social media integration components created and tested separately and within the ESRI's web applications builder
    (Team chose ESRI's applications because more able to configure than have to build everything from scratch, also Algonquin Park has ESRI licensing and wished to develop these tools further in the future)
  • Logo and design layouts designed in Illustrator and uploaded into Webflow
  • Utilizing Webflow developer tools to manually edit coded items to suit desired layout and responsiveness
  • Website layout and structure to contain the solution and showcase the process of development; completed in Webflow and then packaged for deployment in GitHub to meet rubric requirements
    (Will continue to host on Webflow for future display as a group's design and portfolio of skills attained)

Software Used

Even the best laid plans to accomplish a project will have hiccups; we have kept development logs through the whole process to track these. Below we would like to highlight for you the journey this project took us on and the things we learned along the way. Please be encouraged by our process and maybe take away some tips, tricks and insights for your own map, app and web development projects.

The Reality

Our Journey

How we went from Problem to Solution - meeting the needs of the problem through Web Development and Configuration

The Beginning ~ March 11- March 17, 2020

Begin at the beginning and go on till you come to the end; then stop. - Lewis Carol (Alice in Wonderland)

The group started with an idea, to help people enjoying Algonquin Provincial Park, be more engaged whilst using their trails and maps in the park and in their planning; through the use of social media. We knew this was going to be a big solution with a short time period in which to execute. So, to the drawing board!

The group created a rough problem statement, outline with due dates and broke the deliverable into more manageable pieces. These pieces were broken into more specific tasks and based on the individual group member's interests, were assigned. We used Microsoft Office's Planner tool to get our ideas laid out.
‍

As a group we decided on a few extra meeting times, and after dividing tasks we set to researching:

  • User friendly web app development software
  • Social media widgets and development tools to add social media into Leaflet, or other Map application solutions
  • Deep dive on development tools and solutions because it's a slippery slope of learning - this is why we have no photos of Nathan and Garrett they have gone missing in the interwebs and Danielle and I had to create bitmojis to keep us company
  • Data sources for the map we wanted to integrate the social media into
  • The scale and layout of the Algonquin Park map we would like to include
  • Minimalist web designs because Sarah hates clutter
  • Trails information from Algonquin Park, history of Algonquin Park, history of wilderness camping, history of hiking, sustainable trail planning, best backpacks for hiking, best hiking boots for under $100, All trails application, developer notes from All trails...... and how to get out of a Google rabbit hole.

(The Trails App design Fairies are about to hit their first taste of the bitterness of reality....

Getting the Ball Rolling ~ March 18 - March 24, 2020

Week two of Solution creation we started to really get down to work. After a group check-in and discussion with our Instructor we had our dream bubble burst, then brought back to the path of reality. We are trying to integrate social media into a map of hiking trails not develop a full mobile application. We decide to still make an app layout as a proof of concept for future use, because it would also help facilitate more user engagement. We are greatly inspired by All Trails as a very used and engaging trail app that helps people get outside and challenge themselves. As a group we decided to use the Leaflet platform to create the web map app (because Algonquin Park is currently using Leaflet for their Web Map on the Ontario Parks Website), Instagram and twitter as the main social media to integrate, Webflow as the website designer and host for future display, and Github for hosting to fulfill school requirements. Sarah and Danielle are working on the Map and feature layers in another class and will have this available to use for the web map.

But it is time to get down to business!
